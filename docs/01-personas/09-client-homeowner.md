# P09 — Client / Homeowner

**Type:** External
**Reports to:** —
**Frequency on platform:** Periodic — at major decision points, valuations, selections, sign-offs
**Status:** Draft

## What the Client owns

The Client (often the homeowner on JBB's residential work) is the end customer. Distinct from the Architect — the architect is the technical author; the client is the person paying for and ultimately living in the result.

- Approvals and selections (finishes, fittings, sample sign-off).
- Instructions and change requests (often the origin of variations).
- Payment release decisions.
- Practical completion sign-off.
- Defects-period reporting.

## Goals
- See where their project is, in plain English, without asking the project team.
- Approve or comment on selections / decisions in one place.
- Get the close-out pack (warranties, O&Ms, certificates) in one bundle they can keep.
- Defects period — raise a defect and see it close, without chasing.

## Pain points (current state)
- Project status comes through email forwards and phone calls.
- Approvals scattered across email chains.
- Close-out pack arrives as attachments across multiple emails.

## Involvement across workflows
- **Approver on:** 05 (variation sign-off where client cost is involved), 07 (valuation review on their project), 08 (Practical Completion sign-off, defects sign-off, final VAT outcome).
- **Source on:** 00 (origin of the project — may come direct or via architect), 05 (instructs change requests that become variations).
- **Read on:** 06 (live project dashboard, scoped — progress and photos on their project), 07 (Programme Valuation Report).

## Permissions (coarse)
- Read on their own project — dashboard, valuations, close-out pack.
- Approve variations, valuations, practical completion, defect sign-off on their project.
- Write on selections, instructions and defect reports during the defects period.

## Devices
- Mobile + desktop. Often mobile.

## Notes
- Some clients delegate most decisions to their architect; the Client persona is still distinct because some decisions are statutorily theirs (payment release, practical completion sign-off).
