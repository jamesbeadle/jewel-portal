# P10 — Subcontractor

**Type:** External delivery partner
**Reports to:** Project Manager (commercially), Site Manager (operationally)
**Frequency on platform:** Daily — on site
**Status:** Draft

## Goals
- Bid for new work without learning a new tool.
- Log progress and day-rate time on site quickly.
- Get RFIs answered fast so work isn't blocked.
- Keep compliance documents current without being chased.
- Get paid on time and accurately.

## Pain points (current state)
- Paper timesheets get lost or delayed.
- RFI handling slow and untracked.
- No personal view of progress against the tender.
- Compliance documentation scattered; chased manually.

## Involvement across workflows
- **Contributor / source on:** 03 (returns quote against a bid package; uploads compliance documents at onboarding), 04 (RAMS acceptance, induction completion, toolbox-talk attendance), 05 (raises RFIs from site), 06 (attendance check-in, photo capture, day-rate timesheet entries), 07 (timesheet allocation to cost codes via portal), 08 (defect resolution with photo evidence; close-out documentation).
- **Read on:** 01 (assigned drawings at current revision, on mobile).

## Permissions (coarse)
- Read drawings, specs and BoQ sections for their assigned work.
- Write own quotes, RFIs, timesheets, defect updates, compliance uploads.
- Sign on-site (RAMS acceptance, induction, toolbox talks).

## Devices
- Mobile phone primarily. Touch-friendly, offline-tolerant.

## Notes
- JPMS does **not** pay subcontractors. Subcontractor invoices are paid by the accountancy team via AP (Xero) using JPMS work-order and timesheet data downstream.
- Onboarding is lightweight — invite link, not a full account-creation flow.
