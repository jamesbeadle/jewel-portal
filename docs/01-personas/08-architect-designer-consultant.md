# P08 — Architect / Designer / Consultant

**Type:** External
**Reports to:** Their own firm / their own client
**Frequency on platform:** Periodic — at tender, during design clarifications, when responding to RFIs, on submittal approvals, at valuation issue, at project close
**Status:** Draft

## Goals
- Issue drawings, specs and tenders into JPMS through a single channel without chasing.
- Respond to RFIs in-flow with the relevant drawing context already attached.
- Approve submittals (materials, samples, manufacturer details) before installation, in-system.
- Review valuations and the close-out pack on the projects they're on.

## Pain points (current state)
- No shared system; documents pass through email or portal handoffs.
- Visibility into on-site progress is opaque.
- Submittal approvals happen in inboxes and get lost.

## Involvement across workflows
- **Approver on:** 05 (RFI replies, variation sign-off, submittal approvals), 08 (zero-rated VAT analysis sign-off where they advise the client).
- **Source / contributor on:** 00 (often the lead source — architect introductions / referrals), 01 (issues drawings and specs), 02 (issues the tender), 05 (issues design clarifications), 08 (involved in handover where their specifications matter).
- **Read on:** 06 (live project dashboard, scoped), 07 (Programme Valuation Report per Claim Period).

## Permissions (coarse)
- Read on their own tenders and projects.
- Write on tender submissions, RFI replies, submittal decisions, drawing issues.
- Approve / reject VOs on their work; approve submittals.

## Devices
- Desktop primarily. Tablet on site visits.

## Notes
- Architect cost codes thread through every screen and report touching their project.
- Where the architect engages an external QS consultant, the QS takes the P04 Quantity Surveyor / Estimator persona scoped to that project.
