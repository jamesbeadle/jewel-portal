# Delivery principles

Design rules JPMS is built to honour.

1. **Capture once, flow forward.** Information entered once should not be re-keyed downstream. The biggest specific case is the sales-won → project-shell handoff (00 → 01); the same principle applies between every adjacent lifecycle stage.
2. **Project management, not back-office.** JPMS owns project lifecycle, not accountancy, payroll, HR or facilities. Where downstream tools (Xero, Brightpay, Dext, Chaser HQ) need JPMS data, JPMS publishes; it does not absorb their work.
3. **Right role, right control.** H&SO owns the framework; Site Manager confirms it physically on site; OCC files the paperwork. PM owns coordination; QS owns commercial. These boundaries are reflected in the system, not implicit.
4. **Hard gates where they matter.** Mobilisation cannot move to live delivery without a clean H&S checklist. Subcontractors cannot be awarded with expired compliance. Timesheets cannot be allocated to a cost code with no remaining budget without an audited resolution.
5. **The golden thread.** Every inspection, signature, timestamp and photo ties back to the project record. Compliance evidence is defensible at any moment.
6. **Two outputs, one source.** The Programme Valuation Report and the cashflow forecast both come from project data alone. No source merging from external accountancy systems.
7. **Mobile-first where work happens.** Site Manager, Foreman, Subcontractor and H&SO inspections are mobile-first. Touch-friendly, offline-tolerant, low taps to capture.
8. **Manage by exception.** Directors and the FD see the exceptions; they don't have to look at the steady state. The portfolio view in workflow 09 is the surface that delivers this.
